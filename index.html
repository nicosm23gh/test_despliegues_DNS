About us

We are Ellectric Callboy , a band driven by high-energy music and a desire to break boundaries. Since 2010, we’ve forged our own path in the metalcore and electronic scene, blending heavy breakdowns with infectious synths and catchy choruses, connecting with fans who love that we always bring 100% to our sound.

In recent years, our impact has grown with tracks like "Pump It" and our cover of "Everytime We Touch." "Pump It" mixes intense metalcore with an upbeat workout vibe, while our take on "Everytime We Touch" brought new life to a classic pop hit. These songs have helped us stand out and strengthen our connection with fans.

Biography

Electric Callboy is a German electronicore band formed in 2010 in Castrop-Rauxel. Originally known as Eskimo Callboy, they blended metalcore, post-hardcore, and electronic dance music (EDM) to create their unique sound. Their early releases, including Bury Me in Vegas (2012) and We Are the Mess (2014), quickly earned them a strong following with their wild party anthem style.

In 2020, the band introduced Nico Sallach as their new vocalist, following the departure of founding member Sebastian “Sushi” Biesler. Nico, formerly of To the Rats and Wolves, brought fresh energy, leading to the release of successful singles like "Hypa Hypa", which marked a creative turning point . His addition revitalized the band's sound and live performances.

In 2022, they officially changed their name from Eskimo Callboy to Electric Callboy to avoid any culturallyinsensitive connotations. This name change reflected their evolving public image while maintaining their energetic, playful spirit, helping them embrace a growing modern audience. The rebranding signaled a fresh chapter while staying true to their core essence.

Their latest album, TEKKNO (2022), cemented their status in the metalcore scene with its powerful blend of heavy breakdowns and catchy electronic beats. Featuring hit singles like "Pump It" and "Spaceman," the album was praised for pushing genre boundaries and capturing the energy of their live shows, solidifying their place as one of metalcore’s most innovative acts.
